### **1. Sakura Minamoto: Mimikyu**

Sakura is the ultimate Mimikyu main, and it's not even close.

* **The Psychological Fit:** Mimikyu is defined by its deep desire to be loved like Pikachu, hiding its true, terrifying form under a clumsy, homemade rag. Sakura spent her entire human life trying to copy the pristine idol narrative she saw on TV, only to end up hiding her true, scarred zombie face under heavy makeup.
* **The Gameplay:** Mimikyu's core mechanic is built around *Disguise*—taking a hit without taking damage. Sakura knows exactly what it's like to take a violent, fatal hit (the truck) and somehow keep moving. She would completely sympathize with Mimikyu's tragic, hard-working vibe.

### **2. Saki Nikaido: Ceruledge**

Saki needs something with frontline aggression, speed, and absolute edge.

* **The Psychological Fit:** Ceruledge is an armored phantom that fights with literal fire-blades fueled by lingering regrets and street-level grudges. Its design screams 1990s delinquent cool.
* **The Gameplay:** Saki wants to be right in the middle of a chaotic team fight, dealing massive physical damage and using high-mobility dashes to chase down opponents. She would find Ceruledge's fast-paced, ruthless, fiery combat style incredibly fun and natural for her twitch reflexes.

### **3. Ai Mizuno: Decidueye**

Ai is the perfectionist who demands absolute mechanical precision and spatial awareness.

* **The Psychological Fit:** Decidueye is a literal ghost archer (*Spirit Shackle*) that requires flawless positioning. It is a fragile "glass cannon"—if an enemy gets too close, it gets deleted. This matches Ai's Heisei professional mindset: flawless execution from a safe, calculated distance.
* **The Gameplay:** As a center, Ai understands how to direct a stage. Playing Decidueye forces her to maintain perfect positioning, sniping targets from across the map. She would practice her skill-shots thousands of times until her accuracy was a perfect 100%.

### **4. Junko Konno: Aegislash**

Junko represents the traditional, highly structured Showa era, and her gameplay style would reflect that rigid discipline.

* **The Psychological Fit:** Aegislash is a literal royal sword and shield. It operates on a strict, alternating rhythm of offense and defense (Shield Forme vs. Blade Forme).
* **The Gameplay:** Junko would find comfort in Aegislash's predictable, rhythmic mechanics. Unlike chaotic modern characters, Aegislash rewards patience, timing, and traditional spacing. She would quietly master the strict stance-swapping loop, using the shield to isolate herself from danger and the blade to strike with old-school precision.

### **5. Yugiri: Chandelure**

Yugiri brings the vintage, elegant, yet quietly terrifying aura of the Bakumatsu era.

* **The Psychological Fit:** Chandelure is a beautiful, antique, traditional lantern that burns up human spirits to fuel its hypnotic flames. It fits the high-class, alluring, yet hazardous atmosphere of an elite Oiran perfectly.
* **The Gameplay:** Yugiri doesn't want to run around sweating; she wants to control the board. Chandelure is a psychological zoner that traps enemies in place (*Poltergeist*) and melts their health bars from a comfortable, dignified distance. Yugiri would calmly sip her tea while trapping overextended enemies in her flames, completely reading their panic movements.

### **6. Lily Hoshikawa: Hoopa**

Lily is all about chaotic, playful utility and manipulating the rules of the map.

* **The Psychological Fit:** Hoopa is a mischievous, dimension-hopping gremlin that loves attention, tricks, and sparkling things. It looks cute, but it secretly holds massive, unbound power.
* **The Gameplay:** Lily would play Hoopa just to mess with everyone. She would use *Hyperspace Hole* to teleport her teammates across the map right when they don't expect it, giggling as she saves them from a bad fight or accidentally drops them into enemy territory. She would love turning the standard rules of the game upside down.

### **7. Tae Yamada (The Legendary No. 0): Gengar**

Tae cannot comprehend complex button combos or macro-strategy, so she needs a character driven entirely by raw, chaotic instinct.

* **The Psychological Fit:** Gengar is a round, purple shadow balloon that thrives on biting, licking, and jumping out of nowhere to scare people. It is the absolute embodiment of pure zombie energy.
* **The Gameplay:** Tae would accidentally be a terrifying Gengar player. She would use *Hex* and *Sludge Bomb* simply because she likes the spammy, button-mashing rhythm. She would spend the entire game hiding in bushes, jumping out to "bite" unsuspecting targets, and chasing enemies across the map based entirely on a whim.
