---

## Phase Breakdown (Manga)

### **Chapters 1–30: Baseline Failure**

* Pure pre-recovery.
* No learning loop.
* Self-sabotage dominates.
* This is what the anime almost exclusively covers.

No improvement here. Only data.

---

### **Chapters 31–45: Micro-adjustments (Not Recovery Yet)**

* Tomoko begins:

  * Observing others more accurately
  * Making fewer fantasy-based decisions
* Still fails socially.
* Still emotionally volatile.

Important:
This is **calibration**, not improvement.

---

### **Chapters ~46–55: First Real Inflection Point**

**Culture Festival arc**

Key changes:

* She participates instead of withdrawing.
* Interacts repeatedly with the same people.
* Experiences *functional discomfort* instead of pure humiliation.
* Begins forming proto-connections.

This is the **first time behavior changes persist across chapters**.

This is usually where readers say:

> “Okay, she’s not stuck anymore.”

---

### **Chapters ~56–70: Early Recovery Phase**

* Stable peer presence begins.
* Fewer catastrophic spirals.
* Social exposure increases voluntarily.
* Failure still exists, but does not reset progress.

This is where:

* Her anxiety remains
* Her competence slowly rises

This is **realistic improvement**, not wish fulfillment.

---

### **Chapter ~70 onward: Structural Improvement**

* Recurring friend group
* Predictable social roles
* Less internal monologue dominance
* External events matter more than internal fantasy

From here on:

* Tomoko is no longer defined by isolation
* Regression still happens, but it’s bounded

This is the point where **anime-only impressions become objectively wrong**.
