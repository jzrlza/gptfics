You want the **most common, high-frequency Chinese characters and words** for daily survival—the ones that actually appear in 80% of everyday conversations. No fluff, no rare literary characters. 

I’ve organized this into **tiers of importance** and **real-life scenarios**. Master these, and you can survive anything from ordering food to asking for directions to making small talk.

## FINAL SURVIVAL TIPS

1. **Learn Characters, Not Just Pinyin:** Pinyin is a tool, not a language. 水 and 睡 are both "shui" but mean completely different things. Learn the characters.

2. **Focus on Tones from Day 1:** 妈 (mā = mom) vs. 马 (mǎ = horse) vs. 骂 (mà = scold). Wrong tone = wrong word.

3. **Use Spaced Repetition (Anki):** Don't cram. Review these words daily for 10 minutes.

4. **Speak Early:** These 500+ words will cover 80% of daily life. Start using them immediately.

5. **Pattern > Individual Words:** Learn these words in sentences, not in isolation. 我 + 要 + 咖啡 = "I want coffee" is more useful than just memorizing 我, 要, 咖啡.
