## 1. WHAT IS/ARE? (Asking for identity, definition, or category)

**Key Word:** 什么 (shénme) = what

---

### Rule 1: Basic "What is X?"
**Pattern:** X + 是 + 什么？
- 这是什么？(Zhè shì shénme?) = What is this?
- 那是什么？(Nà shì shénme?) = What is that?

---

### Rule 2: "What is X?" (with a known subject)
**Pattern:** Subject + 是 + 什么？
- 你的工作是什么？(Nǐ de gōngzuò shì shénme?) = What is your job?
- 她的名字是什么？(Tā de míngzi shì shénme?) = What is her name?

---

### Rule 3: "What kind of X?"
**Pattern:** 什么 + Noun
- 你喜欢什么书？(Nǐ xǐhuān shénme shū?) = What (kind of) books do you like?
- 你喝什么咖啡？(Nǐ hē shénme kāfēi?) = What coffee do you drink?

---

### Rule 4: "What are you doing?" (Action in progress)
**Pattern:** Subject + 在 + 做什么？
- 你在做什么？(Nǐ zài zuò shénme?) = What are you doing?
- 他在看什么？(Tā zài kàn shénme?) = What is he watching?

---

### Rule 5: "What happened?" (Past/Completed action)
**Pattern:** Subject + 怎么了？(zěnme le?) OR 发生了什么事？(fāshēng le shénme shì?)
- 你怎么了？(Nǐ zěnme le?) = What happened to you? / What's wrong?
- 发生了什么事？(Fāshēng le shénme shì?) = What happened?

---

### Rule 6: "What time?" (Specific time)
**Pattern:** 几点 (jǐ diǎn) for clock time | 什么时候 (shénme shíhou) for general time
- 现在几点？(Xiànzài jǐ diǎn?) = What time is it now?
- 你什么时候来？(Nǐ shénme shíhou lái?) = What time / When are you coming?

---

### Rule 7: "What for?" / "Why?"
**Pattern:** 为什么 (wèishénme) = why (literally "for what")
- 你为什么不去？(Nǐ wèishénme bù qù?) = Why aren't you going? (What for are you not going?)

---

### Rule 8: Plural "what are" — NO CHANGE
Chinese doesn't change for plural. The same rules apply.
- 这些是什么？(Zhèxiē shì shénme?) = What are these?
- 那些是什么？(Nàxiē shì shénme?) = What are those?

---

### Rule 9: "What do you think?"
**Pattern:** 你觉得怎么样？(Nǐ juéde zěnmeyàng?) = What do you think? / How do you feel about it?
- 你觉得这个电影怎么样？(Nǐ juéde zhège diànyǐng zěnmeyàng?) = What do you think of this movie?

---

### Rule 10: "What should I/we do?"
**Pattern:** 怎么办？(zěnme bàn?) = What to do? / How to handle it?
- 我们怎么办？(Wǒmen zěnme bàn?) = What should we do?

---

## 2. HOW FEEL? / LIKE / DISLIKE / HAPPY / ANGRY

**Key Words:** 
- 喜欢 (xǐhuān) = like
- 不喜欢 (bù xǐhuān) = don't like
- 讨厌 (tǎoyàn) = hate (strong)
- 恨 (hèn) = hate (deep, serious—use rarely)
- 开心 (kāixīn) = happy
- 高兴 (gāoxìng) = happy/glad (slightly more formal)
- 生气 (shēngqì) = angry
- 难过 (nánguò) = sad
- 累 (lèi) = tired
- 担心 (dānxīn) = worried
- 害怕 (hàipà) = scared / afraid
- 紧张 (jǐnzhāng) = nervous

---

### Rule 1: Feelings as ADJECTIVES (happy, angry, tired, sad)
**CRITICAL:** Do NOT use 是. Use 很 as a neutral link, or 非常 (very), 好 (so), 超级 (super) for emphasis.

**Pattern:** Subject + 很/非常/好 + Adjective
- 我很开心。(Wǒ hěn kāixīn.) = I am happy.
- 他非常生气。(Tā fēicháng shēngqì.) = He is very angry.
- 我好累啊！(Wǒ hǎo lèi a!) = I'm so tired!
- 她很难过。(Tā hěn nánguò.) = She is sad.

**For questions:** Remove 很. Use 吗 or A-not-A.
- 你开心吗？(Nǐ kāixīn ma?) = Are you happy?
- 你累不累？(Nǐ lèi bu lèi?) = Are you tired or not?

**For negative:** Use 不 before the adjective.
- 我不开心。(Wǒ bù kāixīn.) = I am not happy.
- 他不累。(Tā bù lèi.) = He is not tired.

---

### Rule 2: Feelings as VERBS (like, dislike, hate, worry, fear)
These act like normal verbs. Put 不 before for negative.

**Pattern:** Subject + Verb + Object
- 我喜欢咖啡。(Wǒ xǐhuān kāfēi.) = I like coffee.
- 我不喜欢Pokemon。(Wǒ bù xǐhuān Pokemon.) = I don't like Pokemon.
- 我讨厌这个游戏。(Wǒ tǎoyàn zhège yóuxì.) = I hate this game.
- 我担心你。(Wǒ dānxīn nǐ.) = I'm worried about you.
- 他害怕狗。(Tā hàipà gǒu.) = He is afraid of dogs.

**For past:** Add 了 after the verb.
- 我以前喜欢他。(Wǒ yǐqián xǐhuān tā.) = I used to like him.
- 我讨厌了那个工作。(Wǒ tǎoyàn le nàge gōngzuò.) = I came to hate that job.

---

### Rule 3: "How do you feel?" (General)
**Pattern:** 你感觉怎么样？(Nǐ gǎnjué zěnmeyàng?) = How do you feel? (Physical/emotional)
- 你感觉怎么样？(Nǐ gǎnjué zěnmeyàng?) = How do you feel?

**Pattern:** 你觉得怎么样？(Nǐ juéde zěnmeyàng?) = What do you think? / How do you feel about it?

---

### Rule 4: "Are you angry with me?"
**Pattern:** 你生我的气吗？(Nǐ shēng wǒ de qì ma?) = Are you angry with me?
*(Note: 生气 splits—生 + person + 的气)*

---

### Rule 5: "Make me happy" (Causing feelings)
**Pattern:** Subject + 让 + Person + Feeling-Adjective
- 你让我开心。(Nǐ ràng wǒ kāixīn.) = You make me happy.
- 这件事让他很生气。(Zhè jiàn shì ràng tā hěn shēngqì.) = This matter makes him very angry.

---

### Rule 6: Intensifiers for feelings

| Word | Pinyin | Intensity | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 有点 | yǒudiǎn | a little | 我有点累。= I'm a little tired. |
| 很 | hěn | neutral/very | 我很开心。= I'm happy. |
| 非常 | fēicháng | very (formal) | 我非常喜欢。= I really like it. |
| 好 | hǎo | so (casual) | 我好饿！= I'm so hungry! |
| 超级 | chāojí | super (slang) | 超级讨厌！= Super hate it! |
| 太...了 | tài...le | too / extremely | 太好了！= Great! / Too good! |

---

### Rule 7: "I feel like doing X"
**Pattern:** 我想 + Verb (想 = want to / feel like)
- 我想吃冰淇淋。(Wǒ xiǎng chī bīngqílín.) = I feel like eating ice cream.
- 我想去公园。(Wǒ xiǎng qù gōngyuán.) = I feel like going to the park.

---

## 3. WHERE / POSITION (Location)

**Key Words:**
- 在 (zài) = at / in / on (location verb)
- 哪儿 (nǎr) / 哪里 (nǎlǐ) = where
- Position words: 上 (on), 下 (under), 里 (inside), 外 (outside), 前 (in front), 后 (behind), 左 (left), 右 (right), 旁边 (next to), 对面 (opposite), 中间 (between/middle)

---

### Rule 1: 在 is a VERB meaning "to be located at"
**Pattern:** Subject + 在 + Place
- 我在家。(Wǒ zài jiā.) = I am at home.
- 他在北京。(Tā zài Běijīng.) = He is in Beijing.
- 书在桌子上。(Shū zài zhuōzi shàng.) = The book is on the table.

**For negative:** 不在 = not at
- 我不在家。(Wǒ bù zài jiā.) = I am not at home.
- 他不在办公室。(Tā bù zài bàngōngshì.) = He is not in the office.

---

### Rule 2: Place MUST come BEFORE the action verb
**CRITICAL:** In English, place comes after the verb ("I eat **at home**"). In Chinese, place comes **before** the verb.

**Pattern:** Subject + 在 + Place + Action
- 我在家吃饭。(Wǒ zài jiā chīfàn.) = I eat at home. (I **at home** eat).
- 他在学校学习。(Tā zài xuéxiào xuéxí.) = He studies at school.
- 我在星巴克喝咖啡。(Wǒ zài Xīngbākè hē kāfēi.) = I drink coffee at Starbucks.

**Wrong:** ~~我吃饭在家。~~ ❌
**Wrong:** ~~我喝咖啡在星巴克。~~ ❌

---

### Rule 3: Precise position with location words
**Pattern:** 在 + Place + Position-Word

| Position | Chinese | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| On | 上 (shàng) | 在桌子**上** | On the table |
| Under | 下 (xià) | 在床**下** | Under the bed |
| Inside | 里 (lǐ) | 在房间**里** | Inside the room |
| Outside | 外 (wài) | 在门**外** | Outside the door |
| In front | 前 (qián) | 在商店**前** | In front of the store |
| Behind | 后 (hòu) | 在大楼**后** | Behind the building |
| Left | 左 (zuǒ) | 在**左边** | On the left |
| Right | 右 (yòu) | 在**右边** | On the right |
| Next to | 旁边 (pángbiān) | 在我**旁边** | Next to me |
| Opposite | 对面 (duìmiàn) | 在银行**对面** | Opposite the bank |
| Between | 中间 (zhōngjiān) | 在两棵树**中间** | Between the two trees |

---

### Rule 4: Asking "Where?"
**Pattern A:** Place + 在 + 哪儿/哪里？(Asking for location of a thing/person)
- 厕所在哪儿？(Cèsuǒ zài nǎr?) = Where is the bathroom?
- 老师在哪儿？(Lǎoshī zài nǎr?) = Where is the teacher?

**Pattern B:** Subject + 在 + 哪儿/哪里 + Action？(Asking where an action happens)
- 你在哪儿吃饭？(Nǐ zài nǎr chīfàn?) = Where do you eat?
- 他在哪里工作？(Tā zài nǎlǐ gōngzuò?) = Where does he work?

**Pattern C:** 去哪儿？(Where are you going?) — using 去 for movement
- 你去哪儿？(Nǐ qù nǎr?) = Where are you going?
- 他去哪里了？(Tā qù nǎlǐ le?) = Where did he go?

---

### Rule 5: Directional verbs: 去 (go to) vs. 来 (come to) vs. 在 (at)
- **去 (qù)** = go to (movement away from speaker)
  - 我去学校。(Wǒ qù xuéxiào.) = I go to school.
- **来 (lái)** = come to (movement toward speaker)
  - 请来我家。(Qǐng lái wǒ jiā.) = Please come to my home.
- **在 (zài)** = at (static location, no movement)
  - 我在学校。(Wǒ zài xuéxiào.) = I am at school.

**Important:** You cannot use 在 for movement. Don't say ~~我在学校去~~ (I at school go). Use 去.

---

### Rule 6: "From" (Origin)
**Pattern:** 从 + Place + 来/去 (cóng = from)
- 我从中国来。(Wǒ cóng Zhōngguó lái.) = I come from China.
- 他从家里去学校。(Tā cóng jiā lǐ qù xuéxiào.) = He goes from home to school.

---

### Rule 7: "Where is X located?" (Formal)
**Pattern:** X + 位于 + Place (wèiyú = be located at, formal)
- 北京位于中国北部。(Běijīng wèiyú Zhōngguó běibù.) = Beijing is located in northern China.

---

## 4. WHEN / TIME

**Key Words:**
- 什么时候 (shénme shíhou) = when
- 几点 (jǐ diǎn) = what time (clock)
- Time words: 今天 (today), 明天 (tomorrow), 昨天 (yesterday), 现在 (now), 早上 (morning), 下午 (afternoon), 晚上 (evening), 去年 (last year), 明年 (next year), etc.

---

### Rule 1: Time ALWAYS comes before the verb
**CRITICAL:** In English, time can go at the end ("I eat **at 6**"). In Chinese, time **must** go before the verb, and usually at the beginning of the sentence.

**Pattern:** Time + Subject + Verb + Object
- 明天我去北京。(Míngtiān wǒ qù Běijīng.) = Tomorrow I go to Beijing.
- 昨天他吃了饭。(Zuótiān tā chī le fàn.) = Yesterday he ate.

**Alternative (equally correct):** Subject + Time + Verb + Object
- 我明天去北京。(Wǒ míngtiān qù Běijīng.) = I tomorrow go to Beijing.
- 他昨天吃了饭。(Tā zuótiān chī le fàn.) = He yesterday ate.

**Wrong:** ~~我去北京明天。~~ ❌
**Wrong:** ~~他吃饭了昨天。~~ ❌

---

### Rule 2: Asking "When?"
**Pattern A:** Subject + 什么时候 + Verb + Object?
- 你什么时候来？(Nǐ shénme shíhou lái?) = When are you coming?
- 他什么时候走？(Tā shénme shíhou zǒu?) = When is he leaving?

**Pattern B:** Subject + 几点 + Verb + Object? (For clock time)
- 你几点起床？(Nǐ jǐ diǎn qǐchuáng?) = What time do you get up?
- 我们几点见面？(Wǒmen jǐ diǎn jiànmiàn?) = What time do we meet?

---

### Rule 3: Specific time expressions

| Time | Chinese | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| At 8 o'clock | 八点 (bā diǎn) | 我八点起床。= I get up at 8. |
| At 8:30 | 八点半 (bā diǎn bàn) | 我八点半上班。= I start work at 8:30. |
| At 8:15 | 八点十五 (bā diǎn shíwǔ) | 我八点十五到。= I arrive at 8:15. |
| In the morning | 早上 (zǎoshang) | 我早上喝茶。= I drink tea in the morning. |
| In the afternoon | 下午 (xiàwǔ) | 他下午开会。= He has a meeting in the afternoon. |
| In the evening | 晚上 (wǎnshang) | 我们晚上看电影。= We watch movies in the evening. |
| Today | 今天 (jīntiān) | 今天我很忙。= I'm busy today. |
| Tomorrow | 明天 (míngtiān) | 明天我去公园。= I'll go to the park tomorrow. |
| Yesterday | 昨天 (zuótiān) | 昨天我去了超市。= I went to the supermarket yesterday. |
| This week | 这个星期 (zhège xīngqī) | 这个星期我不在家。= I'm not home this week. |
| Last week | 上个星期 (shàng ge xīngqī) | 上个星期我生病了。= I was sick last week. |
| Next week | 下个星期 (xià ge xīngqī) | 下个星期我考试。= I have an exam next week. |
| Every day | 每天 (měitiān) | 我每天跑步。= I run every day. |
| Sometimes | 有时候 (yǒu shíhou) | 我有时候吃西餐。= Sometimes I eat Western food. |
| Always | 总是 (zǒngshì) | 他总是迟到。= He is always late. |
| Never | 从来不 (cónglái bù) | 我从来不吃肉。= I never eat meat. |

---

### Rule 4: Duration (How long?)
**Pattern:** Subject + Verb + 了 + Duration + (了)
- 我学了三年中文。(Wǒ xué le sān nián Zhōngwén.) = I studied Chinese for 3 years.
- 他等了十分钟。(Tā děng le shí fēnzhōng.) = He waited for 10 minutes.
- 我在这儿住了两年了。(Wǒ zài zhèr zhù le liǎng nián le.) = I've lived here for 2 years (and still do).

**Question:** 你学了多久？(Nǐ xué le duōjiǔ?) = How long have you studied?

---

### Rule 5: "When" as a conjunction (When I...)
**Pattern:** 当...的时候 (dāng... de shíhou) = when (subordinate clause)
- 当我回家的时候，我饿了。(Dāng wǒ huí jiā de shíhou, wǒ è le.) = When I came home, I was hungry.
- 你到的时候给我打电话。(Nǐ dào de shíhou gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà.) = Call me when you arrive.

**Shorter version:** ...的时候 (...de shíhou) without 当
- 他来的时候我不在家。(Tā lái de shíhou wǒ bù zài jiā.) = When he came, I wasn't home.

---

### Rule 6: "Before" and "After"
**Before:** 以前 (yǐqián) — goes **after** the time/action
- 吃饭以前，我洗手。(Chīfàn yǐqián, wǒ xǐ shǒu.) = Before eating, I wash my hands.
- 2019年以前，我在上海。(2019 nián yǐqián, wǒ zài Shànghǎi.) = Before 2019, I was in Shanghai.

**After:** 以后 (yǐhòu) — goes **after** the time/action
- 吃饭以后，我喝茶。(Chīfàn yǐhòu, wǒ hē chá.) = After eating, I drink tea.
- 2020年以后，我去了北京。(2020 nián yǐhòu, wǒ qù le Běijīng.) = After 2020, I went to Beijing.

---

### Rule 7: "From... to..." (Time)
**Pattern:** 从 + Time1 + 到 + Time2
- 我从九点到十二点工作。(Wǒ cóng jiǔ diǎn dào shí'èr diǎn gōngzuò.) = I work from 9 to 12.
- 商店从早上八点到晚上十点开门。(Shāngdiàn cóng zǎoshang bā diǎn dào wǎnshang shí diǎn kāimén.) = The store is open from 8 AM to 10 PM.

---

## 5. WHO / IDENTITY

**Key Words:**
- 谁 (shuí) = who
- 什么人 (shénme rén) = what kind of person
- 是哪位 (shì nǎ wèi) = who is it? (polite)

---

### Rule 1: Basic "Who?"
**Pattern:** 谁 + Verb + Object?
- 谁来了？(Shuí lái le?) = Who came?
- 谁是你的老师？(Shuí shì nǐ de lǎoshī?) = Who is your teacher?

**Pattern:** Subject + 是 + 谁？
- 那个人是谁？(Nàge rén shì shuí?) = Who is that person?
- 打电话的人是谁？(Dǎ diànhuà de rén shì shuí?) = Who is the person calling?

---

### Rule 2: "Who did X?" (Action)
**Pattern:** 谁 + Verb + Object?
- 谁吃了我的蛋糕？(Shuí chī le wǒ de dàngāo?) = Who ate my cake?
- 谁告诉你这个？(Shuí gàosu nǐ zhège?) = Who told you this?

**For passive:** 被 + 谁 + Verb? (bèi = by)
- 这个被谁打破了？(Zhège bèi shuí dǎpò le?) = Who broke this? (This was broken by whom?)

---

### Rule 3: "Who is X?" (Identity)
**Pattern:** X + 是 + 谁？
- 他是谁？(Tā shì shuí?) = Who is he?
- 这位是谁？(Zhè wèi shì shuí?) = Who is this gentleman/lady? (Polite)

---

### Rule 4: "Whose" (See Section 6 — Ownership)

---

### Rule 5: "Whoever" (Indefinite)
**Pattern:** 谁 + 都 + Verb (shuí + dōu = whoever / anyone)
- 谁都知道。(Shuí dōu zhīdào.) = Everyone knows. (Whoever knows).
- 谁都喜欢她。(Shuí dōu xǐhuān tā.) = Everyone likes her.

**Negative:** 谁 + 都 + 不 + Verb = nobody
- 谁都不想去。(Shuí dōu bù xiǎng qù.) = Nobody wants to go.
- 谁都不认识他。(Shuí dōu bù rènshi tā.) = Nobody knows him.

---

### Rule 6: "Who is calling?" (Phone)
**Pattern:** 哪位？(Nǎ wèi?) = Who is this? (Polite, on the phone)
- 喂，哪位？(Wèi, nǎ wèi?) = Hello, who is this?

---

### Rule 7: "What kind of person"
**Pattern:** 什么人 + Adjective/Verb?
- 他是什么人？(Tā shì shénme rén?) = What kind of person is he?
- 你是什么人？(Nǐ shì shénme rén?) = Who are you? / What kind of person are you? (Can sound confrontational—use carefully.)

---

## 6. WHOSE / OWNERSHIP (Possession)

**Key Words:**
- 的 (de) = possession marker
- 谁 (shuí) = who/whose
- 谁的 (shuí de) = whose

---

### Rule 1: Basic Possession (Owner + 的 + Object)
**Pattern:** Owner + 的 + Object
- 我的书 (wǒ de shū) = my book
- 他的咖啡 (tā de kāfēi) = his coffee
- 妈妈的手机 (māma de shǒujī) = mom's phone
- 老师的车 (lǎoshī de chē) = the teacher's car

---

### Rule 2: Asking "Whose?"
**Pattern A:** 这是 + 谁的 + Object？
- 这是谁的书？(Zhè shì shuí de shū?) = Whose book is this?
- 那是谁的咖啡？(Nà shì shuí de kāfēi?) = Whose coffee is that?

**Pattern B:** 这个 + Object + 是 + 谁的？
- 这本书是谁的？(Zhè běn shū shì shuí de?) = Whose book is this?
- 那辆车是谁的？(Nà liàng chē shì shuí de?) = Whose car is that?

**Pattern C (short):** 谁的 + Object？
- 谁的手机？(Shuí de shǒujī?) = Whose phone?
- 谁的包？(Shuí de bāo?) = Whose bag?

---

### Rule 3: Dropping 的 for close relationships
**Exception:** For close family members and fixed compounds, you can drop 的.
- 我妈妈 (wǒ māma) = my mom (NOT 我的妈妈, though it's not wrong)
- 我爸爸 (wǒ bàba) = my dad
- 我哥哥 (wǒ gēge) = my older brother
- 我们学校 (wǒmen xuéxiào) = our school
- 我家 (wǒ jiā) = my home/family

**BUT:** For less close relationships, keep 的.
- 我的老师 (wǒ de lǎoshī) = my teacher (not family)
- 我的朋友 (wǒ de péngyou) = my friend

---

### Rule 4: Possession with multiple layers
**Pattern:** Owner1 + 的 + Owner2 + 的 + Object
- 我朋友的手机 (wǒ péngyou de shǒujī) = my friend's phone
- 我妈妈的朋友的车 (wǒ māma de péngyou de chē) = my mom's friend's car

---

### Rule 5: "One of mine" (Possessive Pronoun)
**Pattern:** 我的 + 一 + Measure-Word + Noun
- 我的一本书 (wǒ de yī běn shū) = a book of mine (one of my books)
- 他的一个朋友 (tā de yī gè péngyou) = a friend of his

**Short form (dropping noun):** 我的 = mine, 你的 = yours
- 这本书是我的。(Zhè běn shū shì wǒ de.) = This book is mine.
- 那杯咖啡是你的。(Nà bēi kāfēi shì nǐ de.) = That cup of coffee is yours.

---

### Rule 6: 的 with adjectives (Modification)
**Pattern:** Adjective + 的 + Noun
- 红色的花 (hóngsè de huā) = red flower
- 快乐的人 (kuàilè de rén) = happy person
- 新的手机 (xīn de shǒujī) = new phone

**Exception:** Single-syllable common adjectives can drop 的.
- 红花 (hóng huā) = red flower (still correct)
- 新人 (xīn rén) = new person / newcomer

---

### Rule 7: "This/That X of mine" (Double possession)
**Pattern:** 我的 + 这个/那个 + Noun
- 我的这个朋友 (wǒ de zhège péngyou) = this friend of mine
- 他的那本书 (tā de nà běn shū) = that book of his

---

## 7. WHOM / RECEIVER (Object of action)

**Key Words:**
- 谁 (shuí) = whom (when used as object)
- 给 (gěi) = to (as preposition for receiver)
- 对 (duì) = to/toward (as preposition)
- 跟 (gēn) = with

---

### Rule 1: Asking "To whom?"
**Pattern:** Subject + 给 + 谁 + Verb + Object?
- 你给谁打电话？(Nǐ gěi shuí dǎ diànhuà?) = Whom are you calling? (To whom do you call?)
- 他给谁买了礼物？(Tā gěi shuí mǎi le lǐwù?) = Whom did he buy a gift for?

**Pattern:** Subject + 把 + Object + 给 + 谁？
- 你把这本书给谁？(Nǐ bǎ zhè běn shū gěi shuí?) = Whom are you giving this book to?
- 他把钱给了谁？(Tā bǎ qián gěi le shuí?) = Whom did he give the money to?

---

### Rule 2: "To whom" as a statement
**Pattern:** Subject + 给 + Person + Verb + Object
- 我给我妈妈打电话。(Wǒ gěi wǒ māma dǎ diànhuà.) = I call my mom. (I to my mom call).
- 他给朋友买了咖啡。(Tā gěi péngyou mǎi le kāfēi.) = He bought coffee for his friend.

**Pattern:** Subject + 对 + Person + Verb
- 我对你说。(Wǒ duì nǐ shuō.) = I say to you.
- 他对我笑。(Tā duì wǒ xiào.) = He smiles at me.

**Pattern:** Subject + 跟 + Person + Verb (together/with)
- 我跟你去。(Wǒ gēn nǐ qù.) = I go with you. (I with you go).
- 他跟我说话。(Tā gēn wǒ shuōhuà.) = He talks with me.

---

### Rule 3: Asking "From whom?"
**Pattern:** 从 + 谁 + 得到/收到 + Object?
- 你从谁那里得到这个消息？(Nǐ cóng shuí nàlǐ dédào zhège xiāoxi?) = From whom did you get this news?
- 他从谁那儿借了钱？(Tā cóng shuí nàr jiè le qián?) = From whom did he borrow money?

---

### Rule 4: "With whom?"
**Pattern:** Subject + 跟 + 谁 + Verb?
- 你跟谁去？(Nǐ gēn shuí qù?) = With whom are you going?
- 他跟谁说话？(Tā gēn shuí shuōhuà?) = With whom is he talking?

**Pattern:** 和 + 谁 (hé = with, slightly more formal)
- 你和谁一起吃饭？(Nǐ hé shuí yīqǐ chīfàn?) = With whom do you eat? (Together)

---

### Rule 5: "For whom?" (Beneficiary)
**Pattern:** 为 + 谁 + Verb? (wèi = for)
- 你为谁做这个？(Nǐ wèi shuí zuò zhège?) = For whom are you doing this?
- 他为谁买了礼物？(Tā wèi shuí mǎi le lǐwù?) = For whom did he buy a gift?

---

### Rule 6: Object pronoun position in Chinese
**Important:** In Chinese, the object (whom) usually comes **before** the main verb when using prepositions like 给, 对, 跟.
- ✅ 我**给**你咖啡。(Wǒ **gěi** nǐ kāfēi.) = I give **you** coffee. (You comes before coffee).
- ❌ 我给咖啡你。 (Wrong order).
- ✅ 他**对**我笑。(Tā **duì** wǒ xiào.) = He smiles **at me**. (Me comes before smile).
- ✅ 我**跟**他去。(Wǒ **gēn** tā qù.) = I go **with him**. (Him comes before go).

---

## 8. TO DO / TO NOT DO / PLEASE / REQUEST / WARNING

**Key Words:**
- 做 (zuò) = to do (general)
- 不 (bù) = not (present/future/habitual)
- 没 (méi) = not (past/negative existence)
- 别 (bié) = don't! (command/warning)
- 请 (qǐng) = please (request)
- 要 (yào) = want to / will
- 想 (xiǎng) = want to / would like to
- 应该 (yīnggāi) = should
- 可以 (kěyǐ) = can / may
- 能 (néng) = can / be able to

---

### Rule 1: Basic "To do"
**Pattern:** Subject + Verb + Object
- 我吃饭。(Wǒ chīfàn.) = I eat.
- 他工作。(Tā gōngzuò.) = He works.
- 她看书。(Tā kànshū.) = She reads.

**"To do" as a general verb:** 做 (zuò)
- 你在做什么？(Nǐ zài zuò shénme?) = What are you doing?
- 我做作业。(Wǒ zuò zuòyè.) = I do homework.

---

### Rule 2: "To not do" (Negative)
**For present/future/habitual:** Use **不 (bù)** before the verb.
- 我不吃饭。(Wǒ bù chīfàn.) = I don't eat.
- 他不工作。(Tā bù gōngzuò.) = He doesn't work.
- 我不去北京。(Wǒ bù qù Běijīng.) = I won't go to Beijing.

**For past actions:** Use **没 (méi)** before the verb.
- 我没吃饭。(Wǒ méi chīfàn.) = I didn't eat.
- 他没去北京。(Tā méi qù Běijīng.) = He didn't go to Beijing.

**For "never":** 从来不 (cónglái bù) + Verb
- 我从来不吃肉。(Wǒ cónglái bù chī ròu.) = I never eat meat.
- 他从来没去过北京。(Tā cónglái méi qù guò Běijīng.) = He has never been to Beijing.

**For "not yet":** 还没 (hái méi) + Verb
- 我还没吃饭。(Wǒ hái méi chīfàn.) = I haven't eaten yet.
- 他还没来。(Tā hái méi lái.) = He hasn't come yet.

---

### Rule 3: "Please" (Polite Request)
**Pattern:** 请 + Verb + (Object)
- 请坐。(Qǐng zuò.) = Please sit.
- 请给我咖啡。(Qǐng gěi wǒ kāfēi.) = Please give me coffee.
- 请说中文。(Qǐng shuō Zhōngwén.) = Please speak Chinese.

**Pattern:** 请 + Subject + Verb + Object (more formal)
- 请你给我一杯水。(Qǐng nǐ gěi wǒ yī bēi shuǐ.) = Please give me a glass of water.
- 请你等一下。(Qǐng nǐ děng yīxià.) = Please wait a moment.

---

### Rule 4: "Warning / Don't!" (Commands)
**Use 别 (bié)** for strong warnings or prohibitions.
- 别去！(Bié qù!) = Don't go!
- 别动！(Bié dòng!) = Don't move!
- 别说话！(Bié shuōhuà!) = Don't speak!
- 别生气！(Bié shēngqì!) = Don't be angry!

**Softer request (more polite than 别):** 不要 (bù yào) = don't
- 不要去。(Bù yào qù.) = Don't go. (Softer than 别去)
- 不要说话。(Bù yào shuōhuà.) = Don't speak.

**Warning about danger:** 小心 (xiǎoxīn) = careful / watch out
- 小心车！(Xiǎoxīn chē!) = Watch out for the car!
- 小心烫！(Xiǎoxīn tàng!) = Careful, hot!

**Prohibition sign:** 禁止 (jìnzhǐ) = forbidden
- 禁止吸烟。(Jìnzhǐ xīyān.) = No smoking.
- 禁止停车。(Jìnzhǐ tíngchē.) = No parking.

---

### Rule 5: "Want to do" (Desire)
**Use 要 (yào)** = want to / will (strong intention)
- 我要去北京。(Wǒ yào qù Běijīng.) = I want to go to Beijing / I will go.
- 他要吃饭。(Tā yào chīfàn.) = He wants to eat.

**Negative:** 不要 (bù yào) = don't want to / won't
- 我不要去。(Wǒ bù yào qù.) = I don't want to go.

**Use 想 (xiǎng)** = would like to / feel like (softer, more polite)
- 我想去北京。(Wǒ xiǎng qù Běijīng.) = I would like to go to Beijing.
- 你想吃什么？(Nǐ xiǎng chī shénme?) = What would you like to eat?

**Negative:** 不想 (bù xiǎng) = don't want to
- 我不想吃饭。(Wǒ bù xiǎng chīfàn.) = I don't want to eat.

---

### Rule 6: "Should do" (Obligation/Advice)
**Use 应该 (yīnggāi)** = should
- 你应该睡觉。(Nǐ yīnggāi shuìjiào.) = You should sleep.
- 他不应该抽烟。(Tā bù yīnggāi chōuyān.) = He shouldn't smoke.

**Use 得 (děi)** = must / have to (stronger)
- 我得走了。(Wǒ děi zǒu le.) = I have to go.
- 你得吃药。(Nǐ děi chī yào.) = You have to take medicine.

---

### Rule 7: "Can do" (Ability/Permission)
**Use 会 (huì)** = know how to (learned skill)
- 我会说中文。(Wǒ huì shuō Zhōngwén.) = I can speak Chinese. (I know how to).
- 他不会游泳。(Tā bù huì yóuyǒng.) = He can't swim.

**Use 能 (néng)** = be able to (physical ability / permission)
- 我能去吗？(Wǒ néng qù ma?) = Can I go? (Am I able/permitted?)
- 他不能来。(Tā bù néng lái.) = He can't come.

**Use 可以 (kěyǐ)** = may / allowed to
- 我可以坐这儿吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ zuò zhèr ma?) = May I sit here?
- 你不可以进去。(Nǐ bù kěyǐ jìnqù.) = You cannot go in. (Not allowed).

---

### Rule 8: "Please don't" (Polite negative request)
**Pattern:** 请不要 + Verb (qǐng bù yào)
- 请不要抽烟。(Qǐng bù yào chōuyān.) = Please don't smoke.
- 请不要打扰我。(Qǐng bù yào dǎrǎo wǒ.) = Please don't disturb me.

---

### Rule 9: "Let's do" (Suggestions)
**Use 吧 (ba)** at the end of a sentence to make a suggestion.
- 我们去吧！(Wǒmen qù ba!) = Let's go!
- 吃饭吧！(Chīfàn ba!) = Let's eat!
- 你休息一下吧！(Nǐ xiūxi yīxià ba!) = Take a rest!

---

### Rule 10: "Try to do" (Attempt)
**Use 试试 (shìshi)** = try
- 我试试。(Wǒ shìshi.) = Let me try.
- 你试试这个。(Nǐ shìshi zhège.) = Try this.

**Use 一下 (yīxià)** = do something a bit / briefly (softens the request)
- 你看一下。(Nǐ kàn yīxià.) = Take a look.
- 等一下。(Děng yīxià.) = Wait a moment.
- 我问一下。(Wǒ wèn yīxià.) = Let me ask (briefly).

---

## 9. TO GIVE / TO RECEIVE

**Key Words:**
- 给 (gěi) = to give
- 送 (sòng) = to give as a gift
- 收到 (shōudào) = to receive
- 得到 (dédào) = to get / obtain
- 接受 (jiēshòu) = to accept
- 拿 (ná) = to take / to get

---

### Rule 1: Basic "Give"
**Pattern:** Subject + 给 + Person + Thing
- 我给你咖啡。(Wǒ gěi nǐ kāfēi.) = I give you coffee.
- 他给我一本书。(Tā gěi wǒ yī běn shū.) = He gave me a book.
- 妈妈给孩子礼物。(Māma gěi háizi lǐwù.) = Mom gives the child a gift.

**CRITICAL:** Person comes **before** thing. Never put thing first.
- ✅ 我给他钱。(Wǒ gěi tā qián.) = I give him money.
- ❌ 我给钱他。 (Wrong order).

---

### Rule 2: "Give to X" (Emphasizing the receiver)
**Pattern:** Subject + 把 + Thing + 给 + Person
- 我把钱给他。(Wǒ bǎ qián gěi tā.) = I give the money to him.
- 他把书给了我。(Tā bǎ shū gěi le wǒ.) = He gave the book to me.

**Note:** 把 structure is used when the object (thing) is specific or already known.

---

### Rule 3: "Give as a gift" (送)
**Pattern:** Subject + 送 + Person + Thing (often for gifts)
- 我送你一个礼物。(Wǒ sòng nǐ yī gè lǐwù.) = I give you a gift.
- 他送了我花。(Tā sòng le wǒ huā.) = He gave me flowers.

**Pattern:** 把 + Thing + 送给 + Person (using 把)
- 我把这本书送给你。(Wǒ bǎ zhè běn shū sòng gěi nǐ.) = I give this book to you (as a gift).

---

### Rule 4: "Receive" (收到)
**Pattern:** Subject + 收到 + Thing
- 我收到了你的礼物。(Wǒ shōudào le nǐ de lǐwù.) = I received your gift.
- 他收到了一封信。(Tā shōudào le yī fēng xìn.) = He received a letter.

**Question:** 你收到我的消息了吗？(Nǐ shōudào wǒ de xiāoxi le ma?) = Did you receive my message?

---

### Rule 5: "Get / Obtain" (得到)
**Pattern:** Subject + 得到 + Thing
- 我得到了一个机会。(Wǒ dédào le yī gè jīhuì.) = I got an opportunity.
- 他得到了很多钱。(Tā dédào le hěnduō qián.) = He got a lot of money.

**得到 vs. 收到:**
- 收到 = receive (passive, like mail/messages/gifts sent by someone)
- 得到 = obtain/acquire (active, like opportunities/results/rewards)

---

### Rule 6: "Accept" (接受)
**Pattern:** Subject + 接受 + Thing
- 我接受你的道歉。(Wǒ jiēshòu nǐ de dàoqiàn.) = I accept your apology.
- 他接受了这个工作。(Tā jiēshòu le zhège gōngzuò.) = He accepted the job.

---

### Rule 7: "Take / Go get" (拿)
**Pattern:** Subject + 去拿 + Thing
- 我去拿咖啡。(Wǒ qù ná kāfēi.) = I'll go get the coffee.
- 他把钱拿走了。(Tā bǎ qián ná zǒu le.) = He took the money away.

**拿 vs. 得到:**
- 拿 = physically take/go get something
- 得到 = obtain (non-physical or result)

---

### Rule 8: "Give to me" vs. "Give me" (Double object order)
**Pattern:** Verb + 给 + Person + Thing (when giving)
- 给我一本书。(Gěi wǒ yī běn shū.) = Give me a book.
- 送给我一个礼物。(Sòng gěi wǒ yī gè lǐwù.) = Give me a gift.

**With 把:** 把 + Thing + Verb + 给 + Person
- 把这本书给我。(Bǎ zhè běn shū gěi wǒ.) = Give this book to me.
- 把礼物送给他。(Bǎ lǐwù sòng gěi tā.) = Give the gift to him.

---

### Rule 9: "Lend / Borrow" (Related to give/receive)
**Lend (give temporarily):** 借给 (jiè gěi)
- 我把钱借给他。(Wǒ bǎ qián jiè gěi tā.) = I lend money to him.

**Borrow (receive temporarily):** 借 (jiè) / 跟...借 (gēn...jiè)
- 我跟他借了钱。(Wǒ gēn tā jiè le qián.) = I borrowed money from him.
- 我可以借你的书吗？(Wǒ kěyǐ jiè nǐ de shū ma?) = Can I borrow your book?

---

## 10. TO MAKE / TO GET

**Key Words:**
- 让 (ràng) = to make/let (cause someone)
- 使 (shǐ) = to make/cause (formal)
- 做 (zuò) = to make/create (physical object)
- 制造 (zhìzào) = to manufacture
- 得到 (dédào) = to get/obtain
- 拿 (ná) = to get/take physically
- 弄 (nòng) = to get/make (colloquial, do something)

---

### Rule 1: "Make someone do something" (Cause)
**Use 让 (ràng)** — causal verb.

**Pattern:** Subject + 让 + Person + Action
- 他让我开心。(Tā ràng wǒ kāixīn.) = He makes me happy.
- 老板让他加班。(Lǎobǎn ràng tā jiābān.) = The boss makes him work overtime.
- 别让我等太久。(Bié ràng wǒ děng tài jiǔ.) = Don't make me wait too long.

**Use 使 (shǐ)** — more formal, often for abstract things.
- 这个电影使我感动。(Zhège diànyǐng shǐ wǒ gǎndòng.) = This movie makes me moved.
- 他的故事使我很伤心。(Tā de gùshi shǐ wǒ hěn shāngxīn.) = His story makes me very sad.

---

### Rule 2: "Make" (Physical creation)
**Use 做 (zuò)** for making/cooking/creating.

**Pattern:** Subject + 做 + Object
- 我做了一个蛋糕。(Wǒ zuò le yī gè dàngāo.) = I made a cake.
- 她正在做晚饭。(Tā zhèngzài zuò wǎnfàn.) = She is making dinner.
- 他在做什么？(Tā zài zuò shénme?) = What is he making/doing?

**Use 制造 (zhìzào)** for manufacturing (formal/production).
- 这家工厂制造汽车。(Zhè jiā gōngchǎng zhìzào qìchē.) = This factory makes cars.

**Use 煮 (zhǔ)** for cooking (specifically boiling/cooking).
- 他煮了面条。(Tā zhǔ le miàntiáo.) = He made noodles (boiled).

---

### Rule 3: "Get" (Obtain)
**Use 得到 (dédào)** for obtaining (non-physical or result).

**Pattern:** Subject + 得到 + Object
- 我得到了这份工作。(Wǒ dédào le zhè fèn gōngzuò.) = I got this job.
- 他得到了很多支持。(Tā dédào le hěnduō zhīchí.) = He got a lot of support.

**Use 拿 (ná)** for physically taking/getting.
- 我去拿快递。(Wǒ qù ná kuàidì.) = I'll go get the package.
- 他从包里拿了一本书。(Tā cóng bāo lǐ ná le yī běn shū.) = He took a book out of the bag.

**Use 取 (qǔ)** for picking up/collecting (formal).
- 我去取钱。(Wǒ qù qǔ qián.) = I'll go withdraw/get money.
- 请取票。(Qǐng qǔ piào.) = Please pick up the ticket.

---

### Rule 4: "Get someone to do something" (Request)
**Use 让 (ràng)** or 叫 (jiào) or 请 (qǐng).

**Pattern:** Subject + 让/叫/请 + Person + Action
- 我叫他来了。(Wǒ jiào tā lái le.) = I got him to come.
- 请老师解释一下。(Qǐng lǎoshī jiěshì yīxià.) = Get the teacher to explain.
- 我让他帮我。(Wǒ ràng tā bāng wǒ.) = I got him to help me.

---

### Rule 5: "Make it + Adjective" (Result)
**Pattern:** 把 + Object + Verb + Adjective/Result

- 我把房间弄干净了。(Wǒ bǎ fángjiān nòng gānjìng le.) = I made the room clean. (I got the room clean).
- 你把饭做好了吗？(Nǐ bǎ fàn zuò hǎo le ma?) = Did you make the meal ready?
- 他把我说哭了。(Tā bǎ wǒ shuō kū le.) = He made me cry (by talking).

**Use 弄 (nòng)** colloquially for "make/get" something into a state.
- 我弄错了。(Wǒ nòng cuò le.) = I got it wrong / I made a mistake.
- 她弄坏了手机。(Tā nòng huài le shǒujī.) = She broke the phone. (She made it broken).

---

### Rule 6: "Make a decision"
**Pattern:** 做 + 决定 (zuò juédìng) = make a decision
- 我做了决定。(Wǒ zuò le juédìng.) = I made a decision.

**Make a promise:** 做 + 承诺 (zuò chéngnuò) = make a promise
- 他做了一个承诺。(Tā zuò le yī gè chéngnuò.) = He made a promise.

---

### Rule 7: "Get + past participle" (Get it done)
**Use 把...弄好 (bǎ...nòng hǎo)** = get it done.
- 我把工作弄好了。(Wǒ bǎ gōngzuò nòng hǎo le.) = I got the work done.
- 你得把作业做完。(Nǐ děi bǎ zuòyè zuò wán.) = You need to get the homework done.

**Use 让...被 (ràng...bèi)** for "get it done by someone else"
- 我让人修好了手机。(Wǒ ràng rén xiū hǎo le shǒujī.) = I got someone to fix the phone.

---

### Rule 8: "Make a phone call" (Idiomatic)
**Pattern:** 打电话 (dǎ diànhuà) = make a phone call (literally "hit phone")
- 我给他打电话。(Wǒ gěi tā dǎ diànhuà.) = I call him. (I make a call to him).

---

## 11. FACT vs. OPINION

**Key Words:**
- 是 (shì) = is (fact/identity)
- 是...的 (shì...de) = fact-emphasizing construction
- 觉得 (juéde) = feel/think (opinion)
- 认为 (rènwéi) = believe/consider (stronger opinion)
- 以为 (yǐwéi) = mistakenly think (opinion that may be wrong)
- 看起来 (kàn qǐlái) = looks like / seems (appearance-based opinion)

---

### Rule 1: Fact — Use 是 (Noun = Noun)
**Use 是** ONLY when A = B (both are nouns or noun phrases).

**Pattern:** A + 是 + B
- 这是水。(Zhè shì shuǐ.) = This is water. (Objective fact).
- 我是老师。(Wǒ shì lǎoshī.) = I am a teacher. (Fact/identity).
- 他是中国人。(Tā shì Zhōngguórén.) = He is Chinese. (Fact of nationality).

**Negative:** 不是 = is not
- 这不是水。(Zhè bù shì shuǐ.) = This is not water.
- 我不是老师。(Wǒ bù shì lǎoshī.) = I am not a teacher.

---

### Rule 2: Fact — Use 是...的 for factual details about past/completed actions
**Use 是...的 (shì...de)** when you want to emphasize **specific facts** about an action: time, place, manner, or person.

**Pattern:** Subject + 是 + Time/Place/Manner + Verb + 的
- 我是昨天来的。(Wǒ shì zuótiān lái de.) = I came yesterday. (Fact: It was yesterday).
- 他是在北京出生的。(Tā shì zài Běijīng chūshēng de.) = He was born in Beijing. (Fact: Place).
- 我是坐飞机去的。(Wǒ shì zuò fēijī qù de.) = I went by plane. (Fact: Manner).
- 是她告诉我的。(Shì tā gàosu wǒ de.) = It was she who told me. (Fact: Person).

**Question form:** 你是昨天来的吗？(Nǐ shì zuótiān lái de ma?) = Did you come yesterday? (Confirming the fact).

**Key difference:**
- 我去了北京。(Wǒ qù le Běijīng.) = I went to Beijing. (Stating the action).
- 我是昨天去的北京。(Wǒ shì zuótiān qù de Běijīng.) = I went to Beijing yesterday. (Emphasizing the TIME—it was yesterday).

---

### Rule 3: Opinion — Use 觉得 (juéde) = feel / think (subjective)
**Use 觉得** for personal feelings, subjective opinions, and casual thoughts.

**Pattern:** Subject + 觉得 + Clause / Adjective / Observation
- 我觉得水很热。(Wǒ juéde shuǐ hěn rè.) = I think the water is hot. (My subjective feeling).
- 他觉得这个电影好看。(Tā juéde zhège diànyǐng hǎokàn.) = He thinks this movie is good.
- 我觉得你说得对。(Wǒ juéde nǐ shuō de duì.) = I think you are right.

**Negative:** 不觉得 = don't think / don't feel
- 我不觉得水很热。(Wǒ bù juéde shuǐ hěn rè.) = I don't think the water is hot.
- 他不觉得这个电影好看。(Tā bù juéde zhège diànyǐng hǎokàn.) = He doesn't think this movie is good.

**Question:** 你觉得怎么样？(Nǐ juéde zěnmeyàng?) = What do you think? / How do you feel about it?

---

### Rule 4: Strong Opinion — Use 认为 (rènwéi) = believe / consider
**Use 认为** for more formal, strongly held opinions or beliefs (closer to "I believe" or "I consider").

**Pattern:** Subject + 认为 + Clause
- 我认为这个问题很重要。(Wǒ rènwéi zhège wèntí hěn zhòngyào.) = I believe this issue is very important.
- 他认为我们应该去。(Tā rènwéi wǒmen yīnggāi qù.) = He believes we should go.
- 大多数人都认为他是对的。(Dàduōshù rén dōu rènwéi tā shì duì de.) = Most people believe he is right.

**Difference:**
- 觉得 (juéde) = casual, "I think/feel" (less certain)
- 认为 (rènwéi) = formal, "I believe/consider" (more certain, reasoned)

---

### Rule 5: Mistaken Opinion — Use 以为 (yǐwéi) = mistakenly think
**Use 以为** when you thought something was true but later found out it was wrong.

**Pattern:** Subject + 以为 + Clause
- 我以为你是老师。(Wǒ yǐwéi nǐ shì lǎoshī.) = I thought you were a teacher. (But you're not).
- 他以为我走了。(Tā yǐwéi wǒ zǒu le.) = He thought I left. (But I didn't).
- 我以为今天下雨。(Wǒ yǐwéi jīntiān xiàyǔ.) = I thought it would rain today. (But it didn't).

**Negative:** 没以为 (méi yǐwéi) = didn't think / didn't realize
- 我没以为你会来。(Wǒ méi yǐwéi nǐ huì lái.) = I didn't think you would come. (But you did).

---

### Rule 6: Appearance-based Opinion — Use 看起来 (kàn qǐlái) = looks like / seems
**Pattern:** Subject + 看起来 + Adjective / Clause
- 她看起来很开心。(Tā kàn qǐlái hěn kāixīn.) = She looks very happy.
- 这个菜看起来很好吃。(Zhège cài kàn qǐlái hěn hǎochī.) = This dish looks delicious.
- 看起来要下雨了。(Kàn qǐlái yào xiàyǔ le.) = It looks like it's going to rain.

**Other perception verbs:**
- 听起来 (tīng qǐlái) = sounds like
  - 听起来不错。(Tīng qǐlái bùcuò.) = Sounds good.
- 尝起来 (cháng qǐlái) = tastes like
  - 这个尝起来很甜。(Zhège cháng qǐlái hěn tián.) = This tastes sweet.
- 闻起来 (wén qǐlái) = smells like
  - 花香闻起来很好。(Huā xiāng wén qǐlái hěn hǎo.) = The flowers smell nice.

---

### Rule 7: Fact vs. Opinion with Adjectives

**Key distinction:** When you state an adjective without qualification, it's often treated as a **factual observation**. When you use 觉得, it's clearly **opinion**.

| Fact/Observation | Opinion |
| :--- | :--- |
| 水很热。(Shuǐ hěn rè.) = The water is hot. (General observation). | 我觉得水很热。(Wǒ juéde shuǐ hěn rè.) = I think the water is hot. (My personal feeling). |
| 她很漂亮。(Tā hěn piàoliang.) = She is pretty. (Factual statement). | 我觉得她很漂亮。(Wǒ juéde tā hěn piàoliang.) = I think she is pretty. (My opinion). |
| 这个电影好看。(Zhège diànyǐng hǎokàn.) = This movie is good. (General statement). | 我觉得这个电影好看。(Wǒ juéde zhège diànyǐng hǎokàn.) = I think this movie is good. (My subjective view). |

---

### Rule 8: Asking for Fact vs. Asking for Opinion

**Asking for a fact:**
- 这是你的书吗？(Zhè shì nǐ de shū ma?) = Is this your book? (Yes/no fact).
- 你是北京人吗？(Nǐ shì Běijīng rén ma?) = Are you from Beijing? (Fact of origin).
- 他来了吗？(Tā lái le ma?) = Did he come? (Fact of action).

**Asking for an opinion:**
- 你觉得这个怎么样？(Nǐ juéde zhège zěnmeyàng?) = What do you think of this?
- 你认为谁更好？(Nǐ rènwéi shuí gèng hǎo?) = Who do you believe is better?
- 看起来怎么样？(Kàn qǐlái zěnmeyàng?) = How does it look?

---

### Rule 9: Mixed Fact + Opinion in One Sentence

**Pattern:** 我觉得 + [Fact-Clause] + 是 + [Fact/Opinion]
- 我觉得水是热的。(Wǒ juéde shuǐ shì rè de.) = I think the water is hot. (Using 是...的 for factual emphasis within opinion).
- 我认为他是最好的老师。(Wǒ rènwéi tā shì zuì hǎo de lǎoshī.) = I believe he is the best teacher. (Opinion + factual statement).

**Pattern:** 事实是... (shìshí shì...) = the fact is...
- 事实是，他不在家。(Shìshí shì, tā bù zài jiā.) = The fact is, he is not at home. (Contrasts with opinion).
- 事实胜于雄辩。(Shìshí shèng yú xióngbiàn.) = Facts speak louder than words. (Idiom).

---

### Rule 10: Emphasizing Fact with 确实 (quèshí) = indeed / certainly

**Use 确实** to emphasize that something is a fact.
- 他确实来了。(Tā quèshí lái le.) = He indeed came. (Confirming fact).
- 这确实是我的书。(Zhè quèshí shì wǒ de shū.) = This is indeed my book.

**Emphasizing Opinion with 真的 (zhēn de) = really**
- 我真的觉得很好。(Wǒ zhēn de juéde hěn hǎo.) = I really think it's good.
- 真的吗？(Zhēn de ma?) = Really? (Questioning a fact or opinion).

## 12. COUNTABLES & UNCOUNTABLES (The Measure Word System)

**Key Concept:** In Chinese, ALL nouns need a "measure word" (量词 - liàngcí) when you put a number, "this," or "that" in front of them. The noun itself never changes. 

- **Countable (in English):** 三本书 (sān **běn** shū) = 3 books.
- **Uncountable (in English):** 三杯水 (sān **bēi** shuǐ) = 3 glasses of water (literally 3 "cup" water).
- **No number:** 书 (shū) = book/books. 水 (shuǐ) = water (no measure word needed).

---

### Rule 1: The Universal Measure Word (When in doubt, use this!)
**个 (gè)** is the "catch-all" measure word. You can use it for most people, objects, and abstract things.

- 一个人 (yī gè rén) = one person
- 三个苹果 (sān gè píngguǒ) = three apples
- 两个问题 (liǎng gè wèntí) = two questions
- 一个想法 (yī gè xiǎngfǎ) = an idea

**Rule:** Use 两 (liǎng) instead of 二 (èr) when counting with measure words.
- ✅ 两个人 (liǎng gè rén) = two people.
- ❌ 二个人 (èr gè rén).

---

### Rule 2: Specific Measure Words for Countable Nouns
Countable nouns in English have specific measure words based on their physical shape or category.

| Category | Measure Word | Pinyin | Used For | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Flat objects** | 张 | zhāng | Paper, tables, beds, photos, tickets | 一张纸 = a paper; 三张票 = 3 tickets |
| **Long objects** | 条 | tiáo | Fish, pants, roads, snakes, ropes | 一条鱼 = a fish; 两条裤子 = 2 pairs of pants |
| **Round/ball objects** | 颗 | kē | Stars, pearls, pills, teeth, seeds | 一颗星星 = a star; 三颗药 = 3 pills |
| **Vehicles/machines** | 辆 | liàng | Cars, bicycles, buses | 一辆车 = a car; 两辆自行车 = 2 bikes |
| **Books/volumes** | 本 | běn | Books, magazines, notebooks, dictionaries | 一本书 = a book; 三本字典 = 3 dictionaries |
| **Buildings/Houses** | 栋 | dòng | Buildings, houses (big structures) | 一栋楼 = a building |
| **Pictures/art** | 幅 | fú | Paintings, calligraphy, maps | 一幅画 = a painting |
| **Pairs (shoes/socks)** | 双 | shuāng | Shoes, socks, chopsticks, hands, eyes | 一双鞋 = a pair of shoes |
| **Sets/outfits** | 套 | tào | Suits, outfits, sets of utensils | 一套衣服 = a suit/outfit |
| **Pieces (of a set)** | 块 | kuài | Cakes, soap, land, watch, stones | 一块蛋糕 = a piece of cake |
| **Letter/Message** | 封 | fēng | Letters, emails (formal) | 一封信 = a letter |
| **Spoken language** | 句 | jù | Sentences, words (spoken) | 一句话 = a sentence/word |

---

### Rule 3: Measure Words for Uncountable Nouns (Containers & Units)
Since Chinese treats uncountable nouns (water, rice, oil) as undifferentiated masses, you MUST use a **container or unit measure word**.

| Unit | Pinyin | Used For | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cup/Glass** | 杯 | bēi | Liquids (water, tea, coffee, milk) | 一杯水 = a glass of water; 三杯咖啡 = 3 cups of coffee |
| **Bottle** | 瓶 | píng | Liquids in bottles | 一瓶啤酒 = a bottle of beer |
| **Bowl** | 碗 | wǎn | Rice, noodles, soup | 一碗饭 = a bowl of rice; 两碗面 = 2 bowls of noodles |
| **Can/Tin** | 罐 | guàn | Canned drinks, canned food | 一罐可乐 = a can of Coke |
| **Piece/Bar** | 块 | kuài | Cake, soap, land, chocolate | 一块巧克力 = a piece of chocolate |
| **Piece/Slice** | 片 | piàn | Bread, pizza, meat slices, area | 一片面包 = a slice of bread |
| **Small piece/flake** | 片 | piàn | Leaves, snowflakes, scenery | 一片树叶 = a leaf; 一片风景 = a scenic view |
| **Drop** | 滴 | dī | Liquid drops (rain, medicine) | 一滴水 = a drop of water |
| **Bunch** | 串 | chuàn | Grapes, skewered food, keys | 一串葡萄 = a bunch of grapes |
| **Packet** | 包 | bāo | Packets of stuff | 一包糖 = a pack of candy |
| **Bag/Sack** | 袋 | dài | Bagged items | 一袋米 = a bag of rice |
| **Bottle (wine)** | 瓶 | píng | Liquids | 一瓶酒 = a bottle of wine |

---

### Rule 4: Group & Collective Measure Words (For groups of countable nouns)

| Unit | Pinyin | Used For | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pair/Set** | 双 | shuāng | Shoes, socks, chopsticks | 一双筷子 = a pair of chopsticks |
| **Set/Suite** | 套 | tào | Suits, furniture, books | 一套家具 = a set of furniture |
| **Group (people)** | 群 | qún | A group/crowd of people/animals | 一群人 = a group of people; 一群羊 = a flock of sheep |
| **Bunch** | 束 | shù | Flowers (tied together) | 一束花 = a bunch of flowers |
| **Bundle** | 捆 | kǔn | Sticks, straw, bound objects | 一捆柴 = a bundle of firewood |
| **Class/rank** | 班 | bān | A class of students | 一班学生 = a class of students |
| **Flock/herd** | 伙 | huǒ | A gang/group (often informal/negative) | 一伙人 = a gang of people |

---

### Rule 5: Approximate Quantity (About / Around / Almost)
**Pattern:** Number + Measure Word + 左右 (zuǒyòu) = about / around
- 三个人左右 (sān gè rén zuǒyòu) = about 3 people
- 十本书左右 (shí běn shū zuǒyòu) = about 10 books

**Pattern:** 几 (jǐ) + Measure Word + Noun = "several / a few" (for numbers under 10)
- 几个人 (jǐ gè rén) = a few people / several people
- 几本书 (jǐ běn shū) = a few books

**Pattern:** 多 (duō) + Measure Word = "more than / over"
- 十多个人 (shí duō gè rén) = more than 10 people / over 10 people
- 二十多本书 (èrshí duō běn shū) = over 20 books

**Pattern:** 来 (lái) + Measure Word = "approximately" (less precise, often for time/money)
- 十来个 (shí lái gè) = about 10 (people/things)
- 二十来岁 (èrshí lái suì) = around 20 years old

**Pattern:** 差不多 (chàbùduō) + Number + Measure Word = "almost"
- 差不多十个人 (chàbùduō shí gè rén) = almost 10 people

---

### Rule 6: "All" / "Every" (Total Quantity)
**Pattern:** 都 (dōu) goes after the subject, before the verb = all / both
- 我们三个人都去了。(Wǒmen sān gè rén dōu qù le.) = All three of us went.
- 这些书都是我的。(Zhèxiē shū dōu shì wǒ de.) = All these books are mine.

**Pattern:** 每 (měi) + Measure Word + Noun = every / each
- 每个人 (měi gè rén) = every person
- 每本书 (měi běn shū) = every book
- 我们每个人都很开心。(Wǒmen měi gè rén dōu hěn kāixīn.) = Every one of us is happy. *(Note: 每 requires 都 in the verb phrase).*

---

### Rule 7: "Some" / "A few" (Indefinite Quantity)
**Pattern:** 一些 (yīxiē) + (Measure Word) + Noun = some / a few
- 一些书 (yīxiē shū) = some books
- 一些水 (yīxiē shuǐ) = some water
- 一些人 (yīxiē rén) = some people

**Pattern:** 一点儿 (yīdiǎnr) + Noun = a little bit (for uncountable or abstract)
- 一点儿水 (yīdiǎnr shuǐ) = a little water
- 一点儿时间 (yīdiǎnr shíjiān) = a little time
- 他会说一点儿中文。(Tā huì shuō yīdiǎnr Zhōngwén.) = He can speak a little Chinese.

---

### Rule 8: "Much / Many" (Large Quantity)
**For large quantities (countable or uncountable):** Use 多 (duō) or 很多 (hěnduō).

- 很多人 (hěnduō rén) = many people
- 很多水 (hěnduō shuǐ) = a lot of water
- 太多了！(Tài duō le!) = Too much / Too many!

**For "how many / how much" (Questions):**
- 多少 (duōshao) = how many/much (for any number, usually when the answer is 10+)
- 几 (jǐ) = how many (when the answer is expected to be under 10)
- 多少人？(Duōshao rén?) = How many people? (Could be any number).
- 几个人？(Jǐ gè rén?) = How many people? (Expecting 1-10).
- 多少钱？(Duōshao qián?) = How much money?

---

## 13. QUANTITY (Amount, Proportion, Degrees)

**Key Words:**
- 很多 (hěnduō) = a lot / many
- 很少 (hěnshǎo) = very few / little
- 太多 (tài duō) = too many/much
- 太少 (tài shǎo) = too few/little
- 足够 (zúgòu) = enough / sufficient
- 不够 (búgòu) = not enough
- 差不多 (chàbùduō) = about / approximately
- 几乎 (jīhū) = almost / nearly
- 完全 (wánquán) = completely / entirely

---

### Rule 1: "A lot" vs. "Very few"
**Pattern:** Subject + 很 + Adjective of quantity

| English | Chinese | Pinyin | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| A lot / Many | 很多 | hěnduō | 我有很多书。= I have a lot of books. |
| Very few / Little | 很少 | hěnshǎo | 他很少来。= He rarely comes. |
| Too many/much | 太多 | tài duō | 太多人了！= Too many people! |
| Too few/little | 太少 | tài shǎo | 钱太少了。= The money is too little. |
| Enough | 足够 | zúgòu | 时间足够。= The time is enough. |
| Not enough | 不够 | búgòu | 钱不够。= The money isn't enough. |

---

### Rule 2: "How many/much" Questions
**Pattern:** Subject + 有 + 多少/几 + Measure Word + Noun?
- 你有多少本书？(Nǐ yǒu duōshao běn shū?) = How many books do you have? (Unspecified number).
- 你有几个朋友？(Nǐ yǒu jǐ gè péngyou?) = How many friends do you have? (Expecting 1-10).
- 多少钱？(Duōshao qián?) = How much does it cost? (Money is always 多少).

---

### Rule 3: "Almost" vs. "About" vs. "Exactly"
| English | Chinese | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| About / Approximately | 差不多 / 大概 (dàgài) | 差不多十个人 = about 10 people / 大概十点 = about 10 o'clock |
| Almost / Nearly | 几乎 (jīhū) | 几乎每个人都来了 = almost everyone came |
| Exactly / Precisely | 正好 (zhènghǎo) / 刚好 (gānghǎo) | 正好十个人 = exactly 10 people / 刚好十点 = exactly 10 o'clock |

---

### Rule 4: "All" vs. "Every" vs. "Each"
| English | Chinese | Pattern | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| All | 都 (dōu) | Subject + 都 + Verb | 我们都会说中文。= We all can speak Chinese. |
| Every | 每 (měi) + MW | 每 + MW + Noun + 都... | 每个人都很忙。= Every person is busy. |
| Each | 各 (gè) | 各 + MW + Noun | 各人都有不同的想法。= Each person has a different idea. |

---

### Rule 5: "Only" (Limiting Quantity)
**Pattern:** 只 (zhǐ) + Verb + (Measure Word + Noun)
- 我只有一本书。(Wǒ zhǐ yǒu yī běn shū.) = I only have one book.
- 他只想喝一杯水。(Tā zhǐ xiǎng hē yī bēi shuǐ.) = He only wants to drink one glass of water.
- 只有五个人来了。(Zhǐ yǒu wǔ gè rén lái le.) = Only five people came.

---

### Rule 6: "Also" / "Another" (Additional Quantity)
**For adding more (countable):** 再 (zài) + Verb + 一 + MW
- 我想再吃一个苹果。(Wǒ xiǎng zài chī yī gè píngguǒ.) = I want to eat one more apple.
- 请再来一杯咖啡。(Qǐng zài lái yī bēi kāfēi.) = Please have another cup of coffee.

**For "also" (in general):** 也 (yě)
- 我也要一杯水。(Wǒ yě yào yī bēi shuǐ.) = I also want a glass of water.

---

### Rule 7: "Enough" / "Sufficient" (Quality of Quantity)
**Pattern:** Subject + 够 + Verb / Adjective
- 时间够吗？(Shíjiān gòu ma?) = Is the time enough?
- 钱够用。(Qián gòu yòng.) = The money is enough to use.
- 不够大。(Búgòu dà.) = Not big enough.

**Pattern:** Subject + 有 + 足够 + Noun + 来 + Verb (formal)
- 我们有足够的时间来完成工作。(Wǒmen yǒu zúgòu de shíjiān lái wánchéng gōngzuò.) = We have enough time to finish the work.

---

### Rule 8: "Every time" / "Each time"
**Pattern:** 每次 + Subject + 都 + Verb
- 每次他来，我都请他吃饭。(Měi cì tā lái, wǒ dōu qǐng tā chīfàn.) = Every time he comes, I treat him to a meal.
- 每次下雨，我都带伞。(Měi cì xiàyǔ, wǒ dōu dài sǎn.) = Every time it rains, I bring an umbrella.

---

### Rule 9: "Total" / "Sum" (Combining quantities)
**Pattern:** Subject + 一共 (yīgòng) + Number + MW + Noun = altogether / in total
- 一共十个人。(Yīgòng shí gè rén.) = Ten people in total.
- 一共多少钱？(Yīgòng duōshao qián?) = How much is it in total?

**Pattern:** 加起来 (jiā qǐlái) = added together
- 加起来一共三百块。(Jiā qǐlái yīgòng sānbǎi kuài.) = Added together, it's 300 yuan.

---

### Rule 10: "Every few..." (Frequency)
**Pattern:** 每 + Number + MW + Time + 一次/次 (cì)
- 每三天一次 (měi sān tiān yī cì) = once every three days
- 每个星期一次 (měi gè xīngqī yī cì) = once every week
- 我每两天去一次健身房。(Wǒ měi liǎng tiān qù yī cì jiànshēnfáng.) = I go to the gym once every two days.

---

## 14. QUALITY (Attributes, Conditions, Comparisons)

**Key Words:**
- 质量 (zhìliàng) = quality (noun)
- 好 (hǎo) = good (quality)
- 差 (chà) = bad / poor (quality)
- 高 (gāo) = high (height, price, quality level)
- 低 (dī) = low (height, price, quality level)
- 优秀 (yōuxiù) = excellent
- 不错 (bùcuò) = not bad / pretty good
- 怎么样 (zěnmeyàng) = how is it? / what's it like?

---

### Rule 1: Basic Quality Description (Adjective + 的 + Noun)
**Pattern:** Quality-Adjective + 的 + Noun
- 好的手机 (hǎo de shǒujī) = a good phone
- 高质量的产品 (gāo zhìliàng de chǎnpǐn) = a high-quality product
- 便宜的衣服 (piányi de yīfu) = cheap clothes
- 贵的车 (guì de chē) = expensive car

**Single-syllable adjectives can drop 的:**
- 好书 (hǎo shū) = good book
- 新车 (xīn chē) = new car
- 贵手机 (guì shǒujī) = expensive phone (still acceptable)

---

### Rule 2: Asking about Quality (General)
**Pattern:** Subject + 怎么样？(zěnmeyàng?) = How is it? / What's it like?

- 这个手机怎么样？(Zhège shǒujī zěnmeyàng?) = How is this phone? (Asking about its quality/performance).
- 这个菜怎么样？(Zhège cài zěnmeyàng?) = How is this dish? (Asking about taste/quality).
- 你觉得这本书怎么样？(Nǐ juéde zhè běn shū zěnmeyàng?) = What do you think of this book? (Quality/opinion).

**Answer:** 很好 (hěn hǎo) = very good / 不错 (bùcuò) = not bad / 一般 (yībān) = average / 不太好 (bù tài hǎo) = not too good.

---

### Rule 3: Comparisons of Quality (Better / Worse / Best)
**Pattern A: Equal quality** — A + 跟/和 + B + 一样 + Adjective
- 这本书跟那本书一样好。(Zhè běn shū gēn nà běn shū yīyàng hǎo.) = This book is as good as that book.
- 他的中文和我的中文一样好。(Tā de Zhōngwén hé wǒ de Zhōngwén yīyàng hǎo.) = His Chinese is as good as mine.

**Pattern B: Unequal quality (A is better than B)** — A + 比 (bǐ) + B + Adjective
- 这本书比那本书好。(Zhè běn shū bǐ nà běn shū hǎo.) = This book is better than that book.
- 他的中文比我的好。(Tā de Zhōngwén bǐ wǒ de hǎo.) = His Chinese is better than mine.
- 他比我高。(Tā bǐ wǒ gāo.) = He is taller than me.

**Pattern C: Unequal quality (A is not as good as B)** — A + 没有 (méiyǒu) + B + 这么/那么 + Adjective
- 这本书没有那本书那么好。(Zhè běn shū méiyǒu nà běn shū nàme hǎo.) = This book is not as good as that book.
- 他的中文没有我的那么好。(Tā de Zhōngwén méiyǒu wǒ de nàme hǎo.) = His Chinese is not as good as mine.

**Pattern D: Superlative (The best)** — Subject + 是 + 最 (zuì) + Adjective + 的 + (Noun)
- 这是最好的手机。(Zhè shì zuì hǎo de shǒujī.) = This is the best phone.
- 他是我们班最高的学生。(Tā shì wǒmen bān zuì gāo de xuésheng.) = He is the tallest student in our class.
- 我最喜欢这个。(Wǒ zuì xǐhuān zhège.) = I like this one the most.

---

### Rule 4: "Too" (Excessive quality) vs. "Very" (Intensified quality)
**"Too" (excessive/negative):** 太 (tài) + Adjective + 了
- 太好了！(Tài hǎo le!) = Too good! / Great! (Positive)
- 太贵了！(Tài guì le!) = Too expensive! (Negative)
- 太快了！(Tài kuài le!) = Too fast! (Negative/Positive).

**"Very" (intensified quality, neutral):** 很 (hěn) + Adjective
- 很好 (hěn hǎo) = very good (neutral)
- 很快 (hěn kuài) = very fast (neutral)

**"Really" (emphasized quality):** 真的 (zhēn de) + Adjective
- 真的很好吃！(Zhēn de hěn hǎochī!) = It's really delicious!
- 真的很有意思！(Zhēn de hěn yǒuyìsi!) = It's really interesting!

**"So" (emphasized quality, emotional):** 这么 (zhème) / 那么 (nàme)
- 这么好！(Zhème hǎo!) = So good!
- 那么贵！(Nàme guì!) = That expensive!

---

### Rule 5: "Better to..." (Advice on quality of action)
**Pattern:** 最好 (zuìhǎo) + Verb = had better / best to
- 你最好现在去。(Nǐ zuìhǎo xiànzài qù.) = You'd better go now.
- 最好不要太贵。(Zuìhǎo bùyào tài guì.) = Better not be too expensive.

**Pattern:** 还是...比较好 (háishì...bǐjiào hǎo) = it's better to...
- 还是走路比较好。(Háishì zǒulù bǐjiào hǎo.) = It's better to walk.
- 你还是吃这个比较好。(Nǐ háishì chī zhège bǐjiào hǎo.) = You'd better eat this one.

---

### Rule 6: "Quality level" (Degrees of quality: High / Low)
| English | Chinese | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| High quality | 质量高 (zhìliàng gāo) | 这个产品的质量很高。= This product's quality is high. |
| Low quality | 质量低 (zhìliàng dī) | 这个手机的质量很低。= This phone's quality is low. |
| Excellent quality | 质量优秀 (zhìliàng yōuxiù) | 这家公司的质量很优秀。= This company's quality is excellent. |
| Poor quality | 质量差 (zhìliàng chà) | 这个衣服的质量太差了！= This clothing's quality is too poor! |
| Good value | 性价比高 (xìngjiàbǐ gāo) | 这款手机性价比很高。= This phone has good value for money. |

---

### Rule 7: "Qualified" vs. "Unqualified" (Meeting standards)
**Pattern:** 合格 (hégé) = qualified / meets the standard
- 这个产品是合格的。(Zhège chǎnpǐn shì hégé de.) = This product is qualified.

**Pattern:** 不合格 (bù hégé) = unqualified / doesn't meet standard
- 这个产品不合格。(Zhège chǎnpǐn bù hégé.) = This product is unqualified.

**Pattern:** 达标 (dábiāo) = reaches the standard
- 我们公司已经达标了。(Wǒmen gōngsī yǐjīng dábiāo le.) = Our company has reached the standard.

---

### Rule 8: "Quality of life / Living standards"
**Pattern:** 生活质量 (shēnghuó zhìliàng) = quality of life
- 这里的生活质量很高。(Zhèlǐ de shēnghuó zhìliàng hěn gāo.) = The quality of life here is very high.

**Pattern:** 生活水平 (shēnghuó shuǐpíng) = living standard
- 他们的生活水平提高了。(Tāmen de shēnghuó shuǐpíng tígāo le.) = Their living standard has improved.

---

### Rule 9: "Better" vs. "Worse" (As standalone)
**Better:** 更好 (gèng hǎo) / 好一点 (hǎo yīdiǎn) = a little better
- 这个比那个更好。(Zhège bǐ nàge gèng hǎo.) = This one is even better than that one.
- 今天比昨天好一点。(Jīntiān bǐ zuótiān hǎo yīdiǎn.) = Today is a little better than yesterday.

**Worse:** 更差 (gèng chà) / 更糟糕 (gèng zāogāo) = even worse
- 这个比那个更差。(Zhège bǐ nàge gèng chà.) = This one is worse than that one.
- 情况更糟糕了。(Qíngkuàng gèng zāogāo le.) = The situation got even worse.

---

### Rule 10: "Such a high quality..." (Degree complement)
**Pattern:** 得 (de) + Degree complement to emphasize result quality.

- 好得不得了！(Hǎo de bùdéliǎo!) = So good it's incredible! (Extremely good).
- 累得不行了。(Lèi de bùxíng le.) = So tired I can't go on.
- 好得很！(Hǎo de hěn!) = Very good! (Emphatic, regional).
- 好吃极了！(Hǎochī jí le!) = Extremely delicious! (To the extreme).

---

### Rule 11: "As good as possible" / "As...as possible"
**Pattern:** 尽量 (jìnliàng) + Verb = as much as possible / try one's best
- 我尽量做得好一点。(Wǒ jìnliàng zuò de hǎo yīdiǎn.) = I'll try to make it as good as possible.
- 请你尽量快点。(Qǐng nǐ jìnliàng kuài diǎn.) = Please be as fast as possible.

---

### Rule 12: "Defective" / "Faulty" (Bad quality)
**Pattern:** 有问题 (yǒu wèntí) = has a problem / faulty
- 这个手机有问题。(Zhège shǒujī yǒu wèntí.) = This phone has a problem.

**Pattern:** 有毛病 (yǒu máobìng) = faulty / broken / defective
- 这个机器有毛病。(Zhège jīqì yǒu máobìng.) = This machine is faulty.

**Pattern:** 坏了 (huài le) = broken / ruined
- 我的手机坏了。(Wǒ de shǒujī huài le.) = My phone is broken.

---
